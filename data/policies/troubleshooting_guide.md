# Troubleshooting Guide

## No signal / "No Service" showing
1. Toggle Airplane Mode on, wait 10 seconds, toggle off.
2. Check the network status page for outages in your area (self-serve, no
   technician needed).
3. Reseat the SIM card if the phone was recently dropped or exposed to
   moisture.
4. If none of the above resolves it and the outage page shows no known
   issue, this needs a technician visit — do not promise a fix timeline
   without escalating.

## Slow data speeds
1. Confirm you haven't exceeded your monthly data allowance (see Plan
   Catalog — overage throttles speed to 1 Mbps).
2. Check for background app updates or downloads consuming bandwidth.
3. Try toggling between 4G/5G in network settings — some devices default to
   a weaker band in low-coverage areas.
4. If speeds are slow despite being within allowance and on a strong signal,
   this may indicate local network congestion — escalate for a network-side
   check, do not tell the customer it's fixed without confirmation.

## WiFi/router issues (home broadband)
1. Power-cycle the router (unplug 30 seconds, plug back in).
2. Confirm the account isn't suspended for non-payment (check billing
   status before troubleshooting further).
3. Check for a known outage in the account's service area.
4. Persistent drops after a power cycle need a technician dispatch — this
   is an account-level action and must be escalated, not self-resolved by
   the bot.

## Billing app / portal login issues
This is an authentication issue, not a network issue — direct the customer
to the "Forgot Password" flow. If they've already tried that and still can't
log in, escalate to account support; do not attempt to reset credentials
through this channel.
